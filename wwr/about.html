History

Rafting as an adventure activity originated in the 1950s in the United States and Europe. It is said that it began to be practiced when a group of explorers and fishermen were descending a river in small boats and rafts along the watery slopes. Gradually, it became commercialized. The first company to offer the service, or the first commercial use of rafting, was in Central Europe, specifically in the French Alps. Later, rafting companies began to emerge in the United States.

But if there's a key date to highlight in modern rafting, it's 1972. It was at the Munich Olympic Games that rafting was introduced as a competitive sport for the first time. Following these games, rafting began to gain popularity worldwide, and little by little, new techniques and equipment began to emerge that made it easier to descend steeper rivers, offer more complicated descents, and offer greater safety.
